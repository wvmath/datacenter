What Linville's calculator leaves out
Before any permit moves forward — six things to demand in writing
01
Ratepayer protection
A binding agreement that grid upgrade costs are not socialized onto residential utility bills — signed before permits are approved, not after.
02
Water permit transparency
Penzance has refused to disclose water usage plans. The legislature rejected amendments requiring water impact assessments. Regional DC growth could reach 200M gal/day from the Potomac by 2050. Require full documentation before construction.
03
Funded decommissioning bond
A fully funded trust covering grid infrastructure, building removal, and land restoration — established before breaking ground.
04
Enforceable local hiring
Binding targets with a funded retraining program and real penalties for non-compliance. A press release is not a contract.
05
Noise and emissions limits
Legally binding decibel limits measured at the property boundary, 24/7 emissions monitoring, and a renewable energy transition timeline.
06
Full public disclosure
HB 2014 shields project applications from public review. As Del. Young noted: “There’s no way to appeal something you don’t know exists.” Require full application materials, exemption terms, and environmental assessments be made public.
Methodology & sources
Tax revenue Matches Delegate Linville's HB 2014 methodology: $10B/GW real property at 60% assessed value + $30B/GW equipment at 3% salvage. Average WV levy rate ~$1.80/$100 assessed. HB 2014 distribution: 30% host county, 10% all 55 counties by population, 50% income tax relief, 5% water fund, 5% grid fund. Source: WV Legislature HB 2014, WV State Tax Dept FY26 levy rates.
Income tax cut Calibrated to Linville's ~15% reduction at 4 GW. WV personal income tax revenue base ~$2.5B/yr. The 50% income tax pool from property tax is divided by that base. Source: WV Tax Dept.
Water usage Evaporative cooling: ~2.5 liters per kWh of IT load (NRDC/Uptime Institute). Air-cooled uses ~90% less. Industry reports project regional data center growth could consume 200M gallons/day from the Potomac River by 2050. AI video generation alone uses ~30 liters (~8 gal) of cooling water per minute of video produced. Penzance has proposed using reclaimed gray water — no binding commitment exists. WV household equivalent: 250 gal/day.
CO₂ WV grid carbon intensity ~0.55 metric tons CO₂/MWh (EIA, coal-heavy mix). Cars equivalent at 4.6 metric tons/car/year (EPA). No renewable offset assumed.
Grid load WV average annual generation ~80,000 GWh = ~9,100 MW equivalent average capacity. Source: EIA State Electricity Profile.
Rate hike Total ratepayer burden estimated at ~$6M/MW. This includes direct infrastructure (transmission, Potomac Edison substation, distribution reinforcement) plus indirect cost pressure: PJM capacity auction prices have roughly tripled since 2022 due to data center demand, wholesale electricity prices rise as more generation is dispatched, and baseline rate increases already in WV rate cases compound on top. Socialized across ~800,000 WV residential accounts over 20-year amortization. AEP WV rate filings in 2024-2025 showed 15-20% increases, with data center load growth cited as a contributing factor. Household bill basis: ~$1,200/year WV average.
Bedington Campus Penzance Management "High Impact Intelligence Center." Announced Feb 26, 2026. $4B private capital, 0 public funding. 548 acres, 1.9M sq ft, 600+ MW critical IT capacity. Grid-connected near new Potomac Edison substation. ~1,000 construction jobs, 125+ permanent positions. HB 2014 allows private power grids and exempts from local zoning. Construction late 2026, initial power late 2028. Penzance has refused to disclose project details.
